# Al-Aqmar Mosque (جامع الأقمر)
**Status:** PUBLISHED_MEDIA  
**District:** Al-Gamaleya / Al-Mu'izz Street  
**Type:** Mosque  

## Historical Summary
Built in 1125 AD (519 AH) under Fatimid Vizier Al-Ma'mun al-Bata'ihi during the reign of Caliph Al-Amir bi-Ahkam Allah. Notable for being one of the earliest mosques in Cairo to feature a street-adjusted facade offset from its interior qibla orientation.

*بُني عام 1125 م (519 هـ) في عهد الوزير الفاطمي المأمون البطائحي والخليفة الآمر بأحكام الله. يتميز بكونه من أوائل المساجد في القاهرة التي ضبطت واجهتها مع خط الشارع بخلاف اتجاه القبلة الداخلي.*

## Architectural Details
Features an elaborately carved stone facade with stalactite hood (muqarnas) niches, a central medallion, and early Arabic epigraphy. The interior contains a small hypostyle courtyard surrounded by pointed arches.

### Key Elements
- Muqarnas Hoods
- Stone Facade Alignment
- Keel Arches
- Kufic Inscriptions

## Stories & Heritage Significance
### Street Orientation Innovation (ابتكار التوافق مع الشارع)
**Classification:** fact
Architects aligned the facade with the existing Fatimid avenue grid while rotating the interior layout toward Mecca.
