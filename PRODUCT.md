# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Minarets of Cairo serves a mixed audience:

- Travelers and tourists planning cultural visits and walking routes.
- Students and independent researchers exploring Cairo's architectural history.
- Local guides using monuments, stories, periods, and itineraries while planning tours.
- Curators and content editors maintaining verified heritage information and media.

Users may arrive casually to discover a place, or with a focused task such as comparing monuments, building an itinerary, reading a story, or checking practical access information on site.

## Product Purpose

Minarets of Cairo is a bilingual Arabic/English field guide to Cairo's mosques, minarets, monuments, stories, historical periods, and cultural walks. It should help people move from curiosity to informed exploration, then from exploration to a respectful real-world visit.

Success means users can quickly discover a meaningful place, understand its historical and cultural context, plan a walk or itinerary, and trust the information, imagery, attribution, and practical guidance presented.

## Brand Personality

Curious, reverent, vivid.

The experience should feel like a travel field guide and cultural tour that lets users walk through civilization. It should carry cinematic atmosphere without sacrificing clarity, evidence, usability, or respect for active religious and heritage sites.

## Anti-references

Avoid:

- Generic SaaS dashboards and productivity-tool visual language.
- Sterile databases that flatten monuments into rows of metadata.
- Tourist checklist interfaces that treat heritage as disposable content.
- Decorative "ancient Egypt" or generic heritage clichés without historical purpose.
- Overloaded card grids, ornamental animation, fake imagery, and AI-generated visual filler.
- Separate English and Arabic experiences with unequal content, hierarchy, or functionality.

## Design Principles

1. **Place before interface.** Let verified photography, maps, captions, context, and source notes carry the identity.
2. **Guide movement through history.** Organize discovery around place, period, story, route, and next action rather than isolated feature screens.
3. **Make evidence visible.** Preserve attribution, verification status, practical freshness, and cultural etiquette where they affect trust.
4. **Bilingual parity is structural.** Arabic and English must share the same meaning, actions, states, and visual priority.
5. **Motion serves the journey.** Use cinematic entrances and fluid transitions to communicate arrival, change, and spatial continuity, never as decoration that delays work.
6. **Respect the site.** Tone, copy, imagery, and interaction must acknowledge active worship, funerary areas, photography limits, and accessibility needs.
7. **Design for both planning and presence.** Support research at home, route planning before a visit, and fast practical lookup while on site.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA.

Required considerations:

- Full keyboard access and visible focus states.
- Semantic links, buttons, labels, headings, landmarks, and live feedback.
- Contrast-safe semantic tokens for parchment, stone, imagery overlays, and status colors.
- Complete Arabic RTL parity, including mirrored layout, controls, navigation, and reading order.
- Meaningful bilingual image alt text, captions, and attribution.
- Reduced-motion mode that removes cinematic transforms, smooth scrolling, shimmer, and nonessential transitions.
- Touch targets suitable for mobile field use and controls usable in bright outdoor conditions.
- Accessible list alternatives for maps and spatial views.
- Clear loading, empty, error, disabled, and authentication states.
- No interaction that depends only on color, hover, pointer, or visual position.
